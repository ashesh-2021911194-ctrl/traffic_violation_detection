How the side-by-side bike problem is handled:

The assignment uses a two-tier strategy:

Overlap-first with horizontal tie-breaking — if a rider overlaps multiple bikes (the side-by-side case), it goes to the bike whose x-center is horizontally closest to the rider's x-center. Since bikes are arranged left/right, each rider's midpoint will naturally be nearer its own bike even when bounding boxes touch.

Distance fallback — riders that don't overlap any bike at all are assigned to the nearest bike, but only if within 30% of the frame diagonal (prevents assigning a pedestrian far away from an unrelated bike).
